Your Academy Blueprint

Two bands, three pillars — a model built for the world students are actually entering

Discovery Band — Grades 6–8

Explore, build foundations, discover what resonates

  • More structured academic time with scaffolded mastery progression
  • Rotate through all available applied studios on a set schedule
  • Intrapersonal focus: self-awareness, emotional regulation, foundational social skills
  • Goal: exposure, curiosity, and finding what lights each student up

Launch Band — Grades 9–12

Specialize, build a portfolio, prepare to launch into the world

  • Compressed academics as students prove mastery — more time freed up
  • Choose a primary studio track and go deep — capstone projects, real output
  • Intrapersonal focus: leadership, financial literacy, independence, adulting
  • Goal: graduate with a portfolio, real skills, and the self-knowledge to navigate what's next

Academic Core

Pillar One
Mastery-Based AI-Adaptive
  • Math, Science, ELA, Social Studies
  • Mastery progression — no gaps, no advancing without proficiency
  • Alpha-style compression using AI-adaptive tools + human teachers
  • Discovery band: more structured time; Launch band: compressed further
  • PA mandatory curriculum requirements satisfied here
  • ~5–5.5 hrs total instruction/day (PA minimum, no more)

Applied Studios

Pillar Two
Project-Based Learner-Driven
  • AI & Technology — dedicated studio AND embedded layer across all studios
  • Coding & Development — the foundation that enables everything
  • Phase in: Entrepreneurship → Art & Design → Music → Media Production → Trades/Maker
  • Discovery band rotates through all; Launch band specializes
  • Portfolio-based assessment, public exhibition, real-world output

Human Development

Pillar Three — Equal Weight
Socratic Learner-Driven
  • Not a side offering — a full third of the experience
  • Emotional intelligence, self-awareness, communication
  • Conflict resolution, boundary-setting, resilience
  • Financial literacy, life navigation, identity development
  • Socratic dialogue + mentorship model
  • Discovery: self-awareness & regulation; Launch: leadership & independence

Consumer Education vs. Creator Education

Traditional (Consumer): Absorb lectures → Memorize content → Pass test → Forget → Repeat. Students are trained to follow instructions. Output: a transcript.

Your Academy (Creator): Ideate → Research → Build → Test → Iterate → Ship. Students are trained to make things that matter. Output: a portfolio of real work, real skills, and the confidence to keep building.
The data is clear: Traditional school teaches compliance, memorization, and test-taking. Employers are hiring for AI literacy, creative thinking, resilience, leadership, and adaptability. Your academy teaches all five. The 52% of college graduates working in jobs that don't require their degree aren't failing — the system that prepared them failed. Source: WEF Future of Jobs Report 2025, Burning Glass Institute 2025, Harvard Business School / Burning Glass 2025.
What your graduates carry that college grads often don't: A portfolio of real work. AI and technology fluency built from middle school. Intrapersonal skills — self-awareness, communication, financial literacy — developed over 4–7 years, not crammed into a freshman seminar. The ability to learn, unlearn, and relearn — the actual skill that matters in a world where 39% of job skills change every five years.
